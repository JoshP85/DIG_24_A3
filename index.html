About Wollongong

Wollongong, informally known as ‘The Gong’, is located just 80kms south of Sydney in the Illawarra region of NSW, it lies on the narrow coastal strip between the Illawarra Escarpment and the Pacific Ocean. With an estimated population of 301,000 residents, Wollongong is the third largest city in New South Wales behind Newcastle and Sydney.

Wollongong is filled to the brim with great activities and attractions to suit any and all lifestyles and interests. With beaches stretching along the coastline and rainforests covering the escarpment in the background. Restaurants, cafes, hotels, and the increase of small intimate wine bars popping up Wollongong has it all.

Brief History of Wollongong

The area was originally inhabited by the Dharawal Indigenous Australians. By 1796 Mathew Flinders and George Bass were the first Europeans to visit the area and the early 19th century saw settlers that mainly consisted of cedar cutters and graziers. By the mid-19th century, the first land grants were made, a military barracks was built near the harbour, the town was first gazetted, and George Brown erected the first courthouse.

By the turn of the century, a new courthouse was built on Market St, gas street lighting was introduced, the first town hall was built and the Illawarra railway connecting Sydney and Wollongong was constructed.

Wollongong Hospital was built in 1907 and in 1947 the City of Greater Wollongong was formed, 1956 saw the Wollongong City Chambers opening. Electrification of the rail line to Wollongong happened in 1985. In 1987 a new council chambers were built, and the original is now an art gallery. The University of Wollongong was established in 1951 and has up to 30,000 domestic and international students currently.

Wollongong's Industrial History

George Bass first documented the coal deposits in the area in 1797 and because of this readily available coal, the region has over the years attracted heavy industry, mainly in steel production. The 1870’s saw the first coke ovens constructed at the harbour at the eastern basin. 1880 saw the introduction of steam locomotives to haul coal from Mt Keira to the harbour.

In 1928, the first steel works was established by Hoskins, a few KMs south of Wollongong. The steel works would have several different names over the years, AI&S, BHP and finally BlueScope steel. The steelworks have grown to become a world class flat steel producer, operating a fully integrated steel plant. Other industries have been attracted to the massive Port Kembla industrial complex, the largest single concentration of heavy industry in Australia.

Not everything was smooth sailing for the industrial history of the region, in 1902 Australia’s worst mining disaster occurred at the Mt Kembla coal mine when an explosion killed 94 men and boys, the youngest being just 14 years old. Two other men died attempting to rescue survivors.

Getting Here

Wollongong is located about 70Kms south of Sydney, weather you're travelling by rail or car, there are many options to choose from.

Rail
  • From Sydney Central Station locate the south coast line which departs every hour.
  • From Sydney International or Domestic terminals locate the T8 Macarthur Via Wolli Creek Line, change at Wolli Creek for the South Coast Line.
  • Travel time from Sydney by rail is approximately 1 hour 40 minutes.
Car
  • The fastest route is by taking the Princes HWY/A1 and M1 motorways, travel time is around 90mins.
  • A more scenic route is along the Grand Pacific Drive, with great coastals views traveling through the rainforests of the Royal National Park and along the Sea Cliff Bridge.
